What is the MSc in Computer Science - Data Science?

The MSc in Computer Science - Data Science at the University of Regina equips students with advanced knowledge in data science, machine learning, and cloud computing. The program is designed for hands-on learning, industry collaboration, and real-world problem solving.

Explore the Curriculum

Cutting-edge Curriculum Designed for Success

The MSc in Data Science program is structured to provide a balanced blend of theoretical knowledge and practical experience. Explore our enhanced curriculum, designed to prepare you for the demands of the tech industry.

  • First Semester (Fall):
    CS 700: Software Development Fundamentals (3 credits)

Detailed Course Descriptions

Detailed Course Descriptions

First Semester (Fall)

CS 700: Software Development Fundamentals (3 cr hrs)

Include modern DevOps practices and an introduction to scalable systems in collaboration with cloud computing.

CS 710: Python & Data Fundamentals (3 cr hrs)

Incorporate more advanced topics in real-time data pipelines and data engineering, preparing students for big data applications in the later semesters.

Second Semester (Winter)

CS 711: Foundations of Data Science (3 cr hrs)

Integration of data ethics and data privacy, aligning with industry standards and real-world applications.

CS 712: Foundations of Statistics & Machine Learning (3 cr hrs)

Advanced coverage of neural networks, including deep learning foundations, focusing on real-world case studies.

Third Semester (Spring/Summer)

CS 713: Applied Machine Learning (3 cr hrs)

Projects focused on NLP and computer vision using industry datasets, with strong collaboration from external organizations for real-world problem solving.

CS 714: Big Data Analytics & Cloud Computing (3 cr hrs)

Include cutting-edge technologies like serverless computing and integrate platform-specific training in Google Cloud, AWS, and Microsoft Azure with an emphasis on containerization (Docker, Kubernetes).

Fourth Semester (Fall)

CS 715: Advanced Data Science & Machine Learning (3 cr hrs)

Focus on specialization tracks. Students can choose between AI for Healthcare, Sustainability Analytics, and Business Intelligence.

CS 716: Communication in Data Science (3 cr hrs)

Advanced visual storytelling using cutting-edge tools (e.g., D3.js, Power BI) and greater emphasis on communicating data-driven decisions to C-level executives.

Fifth Semester (Winter)

CS 718: Data Science Seminar (0 cr hrs)

Extend the seminar series with more frequent industry guest lectures, providing networking opportunities with tech startups, government sectors, and AI leaders.

CS 719: Data Science Project (6 cr hrs)

Add industry co-supervisors from leading tech companies, ensuring students have access to tools and real-world datasets. Students can present their work in industry-hosted events and public forums.
